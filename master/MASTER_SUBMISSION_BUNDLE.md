## Abstract
OpenDigital Twin Builder is an open-source, self-hostable toolkit that enables individuals, SMEs and communities to create interoperable digital twins of physical assets such as beehives, drones, fabrication equipment and environmental sensors.

The project delivers reusable building blocks for asset modelling, telemetry ingestion (MQTT/HTTP), state representation, rule-based alerts and real-time visualisation through a web dashboard and open APIs.

Unlike proprietary cloud-based digital twin platforms, this solution promotes decentralisation, data sovereignty and interoperability by using open standards and enabling full local deployment.

A real-world demonstrator focused on smart beekeeping will validate the system, where each hive becomes a digital twin with environmental and behavioural data.

The result is a reusable digital commons aligned with NGI’s vision for an open, trustworthy and user-controlled internet.

## Comparison with existing solutions
Existing digital twin platforms are often proprietary, cloud-dependent and inaccessible to smaller organisations. OpenDigital Twin Builder differs by being fully open-source, self-hostable, standards-oriented and designed as reusable infrastructure rather than a closed vertical product.

## Technical challenges
Key challenges include designing a generic but simple twin model, handling real-time telemetry ingestion, defining lightweight alerts and keeping the system reusable across multiple domains.

## Ecosystem
The project targets SMEs, cooperatives, makers, fab labs, beekeepers, environmental monitoring initiatives and open-source developers. Results will be shared through public repositories, documentation and demonstrations.
